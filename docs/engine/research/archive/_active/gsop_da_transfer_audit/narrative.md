# GSOP / DA Transfer Audit Narrative

## Introduction and Prior Model

The biological calibration of AxiEngine's plastic microcircuit reached a critical bottleneck during the Learning Proof C4 evaluation. Under the frozen v1.4 calibration parameters, the 2AFC Cue Association behavioral task failed to show any improvement in task accuracy for the normal learning group compared to the control and ablation groups. Furthermore, synaptic weights in the exact-charge domain remained virtually unchanged throughout the trials.

This lack of behavioral movement created a clear tension. The prior research model assumed that the core synaptic optimization rules (GSOP and neuromodulated dopamine delivery) were functionally equivalent between AxiEngine and the legacy codebase. However, the lack of synaptic plasticity dynamics suggested two competing explanations: either the parameters were simply operating at an incorrect scale (the mass-to-charge scaling issue), or there was a fundamental structural mismatch in the learning rule logic itself. To resolve this tension, we initiated the GSOP / DA Transfer Audit.

## Phase H1: The Parity Probe of Slot Update Math

To isolate the learning rules, we constructed a targeted parity probe (Phase H1) comparing AxiEngine's production [apply_gsop_plasticity](../../../../../../AxiEngine/crates/physics/src/gsop.rs) directly against a research-only reimplementation of the legacy [cpu_apply_gsop](../../../../../../axicor-master/axicor-compute/src/cpu/physics.rs) slot update math. By feeding both rules identical input vectors across various dopamine levels, dendritic fatigue states, and axonal head configurations, we measured the exact weight change ($\Delta$mass) generated by each algorithm.

The empirical results of this parity probe (fully documented in the [GSOP / DA Transfer Audit Report](reports/gsop_da_transfer_audit.md)) supported the hypothesis of a structural mismatch. Rather than identical behavior, we observed significant mismatches in both the magnitude and sign of the weight changes across almost all test cases.

## Encounter with Evidence and Model Revision

An analysis of the mismatches revealed four distinct algorithmic differences:

1. **Widespread Competitive LTD Mismatch**: The most critical difference is how inactive synapses (synapses where the postsynaptic cell fires but no causal presynaptic spike has arrived) are treated. In the legacy code, all inactive synapses undergo a baseline depression (`-delta_dep`) whenever the postsynaptic cell fires. In contrast, AxiEngine's production rule leaves inactive synapses completely unchanged ($\Delta\text{mass} = 0$) unless they are explicitly in the anti-causal window or suffer dendritic fatigue. This legacy behavior provides the competitive LTD pressure required to depress uncorrelated pathways and allow correlated pathways to stand out. Without it, AxiEngine lacks the competitive pressure to drive selective learning.
2. **Spatial Cooling**: AxiEngine introduces a linear spatial cooling factor based on the distance of the active head from the dendritic segment, which scales down the base potentiation or depression. Legacy applies the full delta uniformly across the entire propagation window, meaning AxiEngine's updates are significantly smaller on average.
3. **Fatigue Gating vs. Fatigue Penalty**: Legacy gates off plastic updates completely when dendritic fatigue is present. AxiEngine still allows the update but subtracts a fatigue penalty proportional to the fatigue ratio.
4. **Causal/Anti-Causal Co-existence**: Legacy evaluates heads to apply either a causal update (LTP) or a depression update (LTD) per tick. AxiEngine scans all heads and accumulates both LTP and LTD contributions, allowing them to offset each other in the same tick.

These findings force a revision of the component-level research model: AxiEngine GSOP is not a bit-equivalent port of legacy slot math. Missing competitive LTD on inactive slots is a leading **candidate** mechanism for weak pathway competition, but H1 does not by itself prove that this mismatch caused C4 behavioral failure.

## Phase H2: LTP/LTD Event Counters Audit (Wash)

Having confirmed the structural mismatch at the component level (Phase H1), we stochastically simulated a task-like presynaptic and postsynaptic spike cadence over 100 trials (**Phase H2**) to measure the exact event frequencies and compute the Wash Index. 

The empirical results rejected the wash hypothesis. We observed a **Wash Index of 0.0000** for both the Normal and DA-off conditions. Because the postsynaptic spikes in the recommended schedule (ticks 40, 120, 200) occurred after the presynaptic input phase (0..20) had ended, all inputs were purely causal, resulting in exactly `0` anti-causal LTD events. We only observed LTP weight changes on matched synapses.

Furthermore, we found that:
1. **Unmatched synapses remain completely flat:** Because they receive no presynaptic spikes, unmatched synapses remain at 0 fatigue and experience exactly `0` updates ($\Delta\text{mass} = 0$). This highlights the lack of competitive LTD in AxiEngine's production rule: unmatched/inactive pathways are never depressed, so they cannot be selected against.
2. **LTP magnitude is highly damped:** While matched synapses experienced LTP, the total cumulative weight change over 100 trials was extremely small (only +66,327 mass units for Normal, less than 1 charge-domain unit per trial!). This confirms that the learning updates in AxiEngine are highly damped by spatial cooling and inertia.

Thus, under this delayed-post fixture, flat or weakly differentiated trajectories are **not** explained by LTP/LTD cancellation. Remaining live candidates include damped LTP magnitude, missing competitive LTD (H1), fatigue dominance under overlapping spikes (H3), DA delivery timing (H4), and mass→charge scale (H5). C4 cause remains multi-factor until a later gate bridges to behavior.

## Phase H3: Fatigue Dominance Audit

To test whether fatigue can accumulate and dominate the weight trajectory under rapid, overlapping firing conditions, we stochastically simulated a modified schedule (**Phase H3**) with postsynaptic spikes occurring during the input window (ticks 5, 10, and 15).

The empirical results rejected the fatigue dominance hypothesis under the Normal condition. Although fatigue accumulated to a high level (mean `9.63` ticks at postsynaptic spikes) and generated significant LTD updates (1,009 LTD events vs. 426 LTP events), the dopamine-boosted LTP overcame the fatigue penalty, resulting in a net weight potentiation (Net Change = +101,644, Mean Net Change = +145.21).

However, in the **DA-off condition ($DA=0$)**, the fatigue penalty and anti-causal LTD completely dominated, driving weights to net depression (Net Change = -67,674, Mean Net Change = -96.68). Under this fixture, dopamine changes the **sign** of net updates; that is a secondary training concern, not a reason to open another open-ended audit ladder.

## Decision: stop H-gates; T015 competitive LTD landed

H1–H3 closed the diagnosis. **T015** changed production `apply_gsop_plasticity`: no causal hit → full competitive `base_ltd` (unit proof green). C4 is still not re-run. Next is one **network-level** weight differentiation probe under the new rule—not another H-ladder.
